Stage by Stage

Where the RFP Lands

Each step maps a guest action to the touchpoint, the KPI it moves, the system doing the work, and the gap between today and the future state.

1

Discover & Order

Maya opens the Wingstop app, rebuilds her usual group pack, and checks out for delivery.

App · Web · DSP Ease of Ordering Loyalty / CRM linkage
Today

1P and 3P orders live in separate worlds; 3P guests are invisible to Wingstop. Ease of Ordering isn't tracked.

With GX

Order is loyalty-ID linked from the start, so every later touch knows who Maya is. Ease of Ordering becomes a measured KPI.

2

Receive the Order

The driver drops off the bags. Maya starts unpacking for the table.

Store · Courier · KDS/POS Speed · Accuracy · Quality · Hospitality QSC audits
Today

The Ops scorecard rewards speed over accuracy and quality. Hospitality (HYPE) isn't consistently measured, so incentives are misaligned.

With GX

Rebalanced KPIs measure Speed, Accuracy, Quality and Hospitality together — what actually drives the guest's experience.

3

The Hiccup · Moment of Truth

A 10pc and a side are missing. Friends are waiting; Maya's frustrated.

Any channel NPS at risk
Today

1P guests call the store — no ticket is logged. 3P guests are bounced to DoorDash. No unified path, no record, no SLA.

With GX

Every issue has one front door regardless of channel, and it's captured the moment it happens — nothing falls through.

4

Get Help · Self-Service

Maya taps Lil Ranchie in the app instead of calling anyone.

Lil Ranchie · in-app Deflection · CSAT Order mgmt + CRM
Today

No in-app help. The only option is the call center, driving avoidable volume and long waits — while competitors ship AI chat.

With GX

Lil Ranchie instantly identifies the order, understands the issue in plain language, and starts resolving — no phone tag.

5

Resolve · Make It Right

Maya picks a refund for the missing items — and could've taken points or a remake instead.

Refund engine · Loyalty · Live agent Resolution time · SLA Policy matrix
Today

The federated refund model depends on Brand Partners, who delay or decline — leaving Maya with no escalation path.

With GX

The policy matrix auto-approves an eligible refund on the spot, or hands off to a human with full context in one tap. BPs can't quietly decline.

6

Heard · Close the Loop

A short survey in her language pops up; Maya rates the recovery and earns points. Her feedback joins everything else Wingstop knows.

Survey · Dashboards NPS · Response rate Survey platform → Snowflake
Today

A long SMG survey with deliverability issues, no POS link, no reach to 3P guests — and reporting that takes weeks across fragmented tools.

With GX

A short, multilingual, points-incentivized survey feeds one source of truth in real time. GX, BPs and GMs see it and act — improving Maya's next visit.

The Recovery, In Maya's Hand

Why this moment wins the relationship

One screen turns a detractor into a promoter

The hiccup is unavoidable. What the RFP is really buying is the 90 seconds after it — and that's where the design earns its keep.

  • Support-first: no upsell, no ordering detour — it fixes the problem.
  • Policy-enforced: the refund amount and "suggested" path come from the matrix, never an AI guess.
  • Loyalty-aware: points and remake are first-class options.
  • Human, one tap: escalation carries the full transcript — Maya never repeats herself.
  • Closed loop: a ticket, a sentiment score, and the resolution all flow back to the platform.
